Instructions

Pig is a simple game to learn but a difficult game to master. In fact, mathematicians use this game to teach probability in higher-level math classes.

This game can be played with as few as 2 players, and there is no limit to how many players. Although if you have 30 friends, this might not be the best use of your time.

Materials

  • A die (a variant can be played with 2 dice)
  • A paper to keep score
  • A writing utensil

Objective

To be the first player to score 100 or more points.

How to Play

  1. The player rolls the die.
  2. If the die shows a 1, all the points for that turn are lost and the die passes to the next player.
  3. If a die shows any other number, the value is added to the current turn total.
  4. After each roll, the player may choose to roll again or hold their points and pass the die.
  5. Once the die is passed, the player adds their turn sum to their total points.

Variants

Pigs (Two Dice Variant)

This variant of Pig (let's call it Pigs) uses 2 dice. The game plays the same as above with the following addendums.

  • If either dice shows a 1, all the points for that turn are lost and the dice pass to the next player.
  • If both dice show a 1, the players score returns to 0 and the dice pass to the next player. PIG OUT!

Big Pig

This quicker variant uses 2 dice and plays the same as Pigs (above). This variant has 2 changes.

  • If both dice show a 1, the player gets 25 points and can continue rolling.
  • If any other doubles are rolled, the player adds twice the sum of the dice. For example, rolling two 5s gives 20 points.

Swine Herd

This variant is meant for a large group to play Pig quickly together.

  1. All players stand up and take turns at the same time.
  2. One player (the "swineherd") rolls a die for everyone.
  3. After each roll, the swineherd declares the current running total and players are allowed to sit down to hold the current sum.
  4. Sitting players call out their new scores.
  5. The turn continues until all players are sitting down or a 1 is rolled. Those standing when a 1 is rolled lose the points for that turn.
  6. If two or more players score 100 or over in the same roll, they share the win and end the game in a tie.
